# Resume Optimization Boundaries

## Goal

Resume optimization should improve clarity, relevance, and evidence. It must not
create fake experience.

## Allowed Optimization

The skill may:

- reorder experiences based on role relevance,
- rewrite bullet points for clarity,
- convert responsibilities into STAR-style achievements,
- surface measurable results already supported by the candidate,
- align wording with JD keywords,
- highlight transferable experience,
- prepare honest gap explanations.

## Not Allowed

The skill must not:

- invent metrics,
- invent tools or skills,
- inflate ownership,
- imply full-time experience from minor exposure,
- claim AI product experience that did not happen,
- hide material gaps by misleading wording.

## STAR Rewrite Standard

A strong bullet should include:

- context,
- responsibility,
- action,
- result,
- scope or metric when real.

Weak:

```text
Responsible for user research and product optimization.
```

Stronger:

```text
Conducted 12 user interviews for the knowledge-base assistant prototype,
identified three high-frequency retrieval pain points, and converted findings
into a revised onboarding and feedback flow.
```

## Gap Handling

When the candidate lacks a requirement:

1. state the gap,
2. identify adjacent experience,
3. suggest honest framing,
4. recommend concrete preparation.

## Interview Talking Point

I treat resume optimization as product positioning, not fabrication. The goal is
to help real experience meet the right reader with less friction.
