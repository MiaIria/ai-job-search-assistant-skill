# Source Quality Standard

## Goal

The skill must not invent job openings, salaries, benefits, or company facts.

Every important claim should be checkable or clearly marked as uncertain.

## Preferred Source Order

1. official company career pages,
2. official company websites, filings, annual reports, or investor pages,
3. current listings on reputable job platforms,
4. credible news or funding databases,
5. employee review platforms,
6. social media or informal posts.

Lower-ranked sources can be useful, but they should not be treated as definitive.

## Recency Rules

Job postings are time-sensitive.

Prefer sources with:

- posting date,
- update date,
- active application link,
- current company careers page status.

If no date is visible, label the listing as date-unclear.

## Cross-Checking Rules

Cross-check when a claim affects decision-making:

- role availability,
- salary range,
- work location,
- remote policy,
- working hours,
- layoffs or major business changes,
- team existence.

If sources disagree, report the disagreement instead of smoothing it over.

## Claim Labels

Use labels such as:

- confirmed by official source,
- supported by multiple public sources,
- reported by employee reviews,
- weakly sourced,
- unclear or stale.

## Anti-Fabrication Rules

Do not:

- invent active openings,
- invent salary ranges,
- invent benefits,
- assume company culture from brand image,
- copy employee complaints as universal truth,
- hide uncertainty.

## Interview Talking Point

For this skill, source quality is a core product feature. A confident but
fabricated recommendation is worse than an incomplete answer with clear
uncertainty.
